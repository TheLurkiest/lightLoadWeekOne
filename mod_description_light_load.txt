[h1] NPCs/Zombies Light Spawning/De-spawn Fix:[/h1] 

[*] this mod ensures that both Zombie and NPC civilian spawning and de-spawning is a little bit better in a couple of different ways: primarily by fixing a small issue with baseline Bandits Week One that causes NPC population numbers to snowball over time beyond even whatever maximum civilian population cap was set in sandbox settings (putting a lot of extra unnecessary strain on PCs for no reason), and also ensures that spawn/de-spawn distance for NPCs is a little bit closer and ensures that the game prioritizes de-spawning NPCs in remote unreachable locations to help the world feel a little bit fuller. It also [u]helps zombie spawning to feel a little more fair/logical by ensuring that the existing system that vanilla Project Zomboid uses (which Bandits Week One has to use too) doesn't result in situations where zombies just spontaneously spawn in MASSIVE numbers of zombies INSTANTLY for every new building the player walks into, including a bunch barely two inches away from the player when they spawn in[/u] (which unfortunately is how the spawning normally works for vanilla Project Zomboid and Bandits Week One). The changes we've made ALSO ensures that [u]ANY ZOMBIES DE-SPAWNED due to being over the maximum population cap (as well as any reduction in the number of zombies spawning in buildings in the manner we just described) get added to a pool which will slowly get SPAWNED BACK IN very close by to whatever SPECFIC AREA they were DE-spawned from... in a slightly more logical/natural way than they otherwise would (ensuring the zombie population numbers stay at whatever areas the game originally intended for each specific region and that cleared areas stay cleared once every zombie has been killed and the total area-wide spawnable population numbers for zombies in that area has dropped to zero).[/u] 

[h3]This game also changes the way that Bandits Week One determines the maximum number of non-hostile civilian NPCs that are able to spawn in any given area to ensure that the maximum spawnable NPCs/zombies accurately reflect any prior impacts to these numbers made by player (even including impacts from previous sessions if player quits and reloads the game to continue) and to ensure that new NPCs don't spawn in ENDLESSLY the moment existing NPCs die:[/h3]
[*] past a certain point [u]in baseline Bandits Week One the maximum number of non-hostile civilian NPCs that spawn in is based ENTIRELY on a TIMER which simply reduces that max number by a pre-set constant amount based entirely on how many days have passed[/u] eventually reducing that number down to zero if it goes down far enough so that NO non-hostile NPCs will spawn in past a certain date. 
[*] Early on in baseline Bandits Week One the game also uses the expected population density of the surrounding area to decide how many NPCs are allowed to spawn in but by the time zombies show up the amount of non-hostile NPCs spawning in is all just pre-determined by a preset timer which reduces their numbers further and further as time goes on; OUR mod changes that so that the player's actions CAN IMPACT the outcome of how many NPCs survive and for how long (which dictates how many spawn in).
[*] the impacts the player makes are MOSTLY local to whatever SPECIFIC AREA an NPC dies in-- wherein [u]for each NPC who dies in a specific area the MAXIMUM number of NPCs that can ever spawn in for THAT AREA goes down by one[/u]-- however, in addition there is also a [u]SLIGHT corresponding decrease in the maximum spawnable NPCs for ANY/ALL AREAS that the player HAS NOT YET EXPLORED for each NPC who dies nearby in-game.[/u]
[*] These broader map-wide impacts on how many NPCs are allowed to spawn in won't be significant enough to really notice at all until about 100 NPCs have died in-game (somewhere nearby to the player) and there should still be a fair number of NPCs running around even after around 200 or so people have died in-game, however by the time that number reaches around 300 total deaths seen in-game from all combined deaths seen in any given area the remaining NPC civilian population (including any non-hostile NPC survivors) will be effectively ZERO (in any areas that HAVE NOT YET BEEN SEEN YET) meaning that NO MORE civilian NPCs will spawn in past that point for any NEW areas that the player visits AFTER that point. The will still continue to spawn in for an areas that you HAVE visited prior to that point; in order for those areas visited earlier to have their maximum spawnable survivors count reach zero they need to die IN-GAME in the area NEARBY to this location to have the numbers update ONE npc death at time until every one of them is dead.
